# Webinar "Multilevel Modeling in Clinical Research: Concepts and Applications"

[Waylon Howard](https://wwwaylon.github.io/)

## Overview

This session provides a conceptual introduction to multilevel modeling (MLM) from an applied clinical research perspective. The first part of the talk explains why nested data (e.g., patients within clinics) violate the assumptions of traditional statistical models and why this matters. We then transition to the fundamentals of estimation in statistical models, contrasting Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) with Maximum Likelihood (ML). The core of the workshop demonstrates how to build, fit, and interpret multilevel models, starting from an empty means model and progressing to conditional models with level-2 predictors and interactions. Key concepts such as the Intraclass Correlation (ICC), design effects, and pseudo-$R^2$ are emphasized. All examples are available for download.

## Learning objectives

Upon course completion, we should be able to:

1.  Recognize how nested data structures (e.g., patients in hospitals, repeated measures) violate the independence assumption of traditional models and necessitate multilevel modeling.   

1.  Understand the conceptual difference between estimation using Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) and Maximum Likelihood (ML).

1.  Fit and interpret a series of multilevel models, including:
    - An empty means model to calculate the ICC.
    - A conditional model with level-2 predictors.
    - A model with cross-level interactions.

1.  Compute and interpret key MLM statistics like the ICC, design effect, and pseudo-$R^2$.

## Audience & Prerequisites

This session is intended for a general clinical or behavioral research audience interested in analyzing data with a nested structure (e.g., patients in clinics, repeated observations). The talk assumes a basic understanding of linear regression. Familiarity with statistical software (like R or Mplus) is helpful for the exercises but not required for the conceptual overview.
